December 28, 2025 - 130th Anniversary

The Day Cinema Was Born

On December 28, 1895, Auguste and Louis Lumière held the world's first commercial public screening of projected motion pictures at the Grand Café in Paris, France. For the first time in history, an audience paid to watch moving images on a screen.

This humble gathering of about 33 spectators marked the birth of cinema as we know it today, revolutionizing entertainment and visual storytelling for generations to come.

Did You Know?

First Films

The first Lumière program consisted of ten short films, each about 50 seconds long. One of them, "L'Arrivée d'un train en gare de La Ciotat" (Arrival of a Train at La Ciotat Station), reportedly caused audience members to scream and jump from their seats as they believed the train was about to crash into them!

Ticket Price

The entrance fee for the first screening was 1 franc. The event was so successful that within a few days, the basement theater was seeing up to 2,500 spectators per day.

Cinématographe

The Lumière brothers' invention, the Cinématographe, was revolutionary because it combined a camera, film processor, and projector in one portable device, weighing only 16 pounds compared to Thomas Edison's 500-pound Kinetograph.

Legacy and Impact

The Lumière brothers' innovation transformed entertainment forever, laying the foundation for what would become the most influential art form of the 20th century. Today's multi-billion-dollar global film industry, streaming platforms, and immersive technologies all trace their lineage back to that December evening in Paris.
