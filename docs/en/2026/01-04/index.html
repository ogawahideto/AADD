THE NEWTON TIMELINE

1642

A Christmas Miracle

Born prematurely on Christmas Day in Woolsthorpe, England. So small that, as his mother later recalled, "he could have fit inside a quart mug." Few expected him to survive.

1665

The Plague Years

When Cambridge University closed due to the Great Plague, Newton returned home and experienced his "annus mirabilis" (miracle year). In isolation, he developed calculus, optics theories, and began formulating his laws of motion.

1666

The Legendary Apple

According to popular legend, Newton observed an apple falling from a tree, inspiring his work on gravity. While the apple likely never hit his head, this period did mark when he began connecting terrestrial gravity with celestial mechanics.

Opticks

Newton publishes "Opticks," revealing his groundbreaking work on the nature of light and color. He proved white light contains all colors of the spectrum, fundamentally changing our understanding of optics.

1727

Farewell to a Giant

Newton dies at age 84. He was buried with full honors in Westminster Abbey. The French mathematician Alexis Clairaut eulogized: "Newton was the greatest genius who ever lived, and the most fortunate, for we cannot find more than once a system of the world to establish."
