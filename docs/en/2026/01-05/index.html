The Human Element

Joseph Strauss: The Unlikely Visionary

"They said it couldn't be built. Too expensive, too dangerous, impossible given the conditions. But I knew better."

Standing at just 5'3", Joseph Strauss was a giant in engineering. Originally proposing an ugly hybrid cantilever-suspension span to save money, he eventually embraced the elegant all-suspension design we know today. What many don't know: Strauss had never designed a suspension bridge before taking on the Golden Gate.

The Halfway to Hell Club

19 men fell during construction. 19 men were saved by Strauss's innovation—a safety net suspended beneath the bridge.

The men who survived falls called themselves the "Halfway to Hell Club." Strauss's insistence on the net—at a cost of $130,000 ($2.7 million today)—was revolutionary for worker safety. Tragically, near completion, the net failed when a scaffolding collapsed, taking 10 men with it in the worst accident in the project's history.
